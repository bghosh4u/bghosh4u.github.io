Polymers are some particles joined through bonds. They are present in solutions, as melts or as slow-moving glassy materials, as ordered crystalline/semi-crystalline substances etc. Somehow our biological system prefers to have the main constituting and functional units (polysaccharides, polypeptides!) as polymers. Because of the presence of connectivity among the units in a polymer, the dynamics become different from independent monomer particles. The scenario becomes interesting with the incorporation of different interaction potentials (angular, torsional, short-range dispersion, long-range Coulomb etc.) other than the simple bonding.
